# Condition

Some Heron 2.5 vehicles may exhibit a completely inoperative HVAC blower at all fan speed settings. When this condition is present, DTC B1352 is stored in the HVAC control module. The condition may be accompanied by no airflow from any vent regardless of mode selection. System layout and component location are described in the [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: HVAC System Overview](doc://man-heron-hvac#hvac-system-overview).

# Cause

This condition is caused by an open or high-resistance fault within the blower motor circuit, originating from a failed blower motor assembly, part number BLW-7200. The HVAC module monitors the blower motor circuit and sets DTC B1352 when no current draw is detected at commanded fan speeds. Internal brush wear or an open winding in BLW-7200 is the typical root cause. The affected component is described in the [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor and Cabin Air Filter](doc://man-heron-hvac#hvac-system-overview/blower-motor-and-cabin-air-filter), and the code is documented in the [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor Circuit Code](doc://man-heron-hvac#diagnostic-trouble-codes/blower-motor-circuit-code).

# Repair Procedure

Replace the blower motor assembly BLW-7200 following PROC-BLOWER, inspecting the connector and harness pins for corrosion or backout before final assembly. After installation, clear DTC B1352 and verify blower operation across all commanded fan speeds. Confirm no related codes remain stored in the HVAC module. Removal and installation steps are detailed in the [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: HVAC Blower Motor Replacement Procedure](doc://man-heron-hvac#air-distribution-sub-system-service/hvac-blower-motor-replacement-procedure), within the [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Air Distribution Sub-System Service](doc://man-heron-hvac#air-distribution-sub-system-service).
