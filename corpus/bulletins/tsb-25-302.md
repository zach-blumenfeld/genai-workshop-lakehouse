id: tsb-25-302
title: "Heron 2.5: Coil B primary/secondary failure causes cylinder 2 misfire P0302/P0352"
area: bulletins
---

# Condition

Some Heron 2.5 vehicles may exhibit a rough idle, hesitation under load, and a flashing malfunction indicator. The PCM will store DTC P0302 "Cylinder 2 Misfire Detected" together with DTC P0352 "Ignition Coil B Primary/Secondary Circuit" when the cylinder 2 ignition event fails. The simultaneous presence of P0302 and P0352 points specifically at the coil serving cylinder 2 rather than at fuel or compression. Refer to [Heron Ignition Service Manual :: Coil Primary and Secondary Circuit Faults](doc://man-heron-ignition#ignition-circuit-diagnosis/coil-primary-and-secondary-circuit-faults) for the circuit-level description of these codes.

# Cause

This condition is caused by an internal winding breakdown in the application-specific ignition coil, part number IC-2044-A, which produces an open or shorted primary/secondary circuit as the coil heats up. The failure of IC-2044-A interrupts the spark event for cylinder 2 and is reported by the PCM as the combined P0302 and P0352 pattern. Coil application and interchange notes are covered in [Heron Ignition Service Manual :: Application-Specific Coils](doc://man-heron-ignition#ignition-coils/application-specific-coils).

# Diagnosis

Before replacing parts, run PROC-MISFIRE-DIAG to confirm the misfire is isolated to cylinder 2 and is not the result of a vacuum leak or fuel concern. Swap the suspect coil to an adjacent cylinder and observe whether the misfire and the P0302/P0352 codes follow the coil, which confirms an internal coil fault. The full diagnostic tree for ignition-side misfires is provided in [Heron Ignition Service Manual :: Ignition Circuit Diagnosis](doc://man-heron-ignition#ignition-circuit-diagnosis) and the [Heron Ignition Service Manual :: Ignition Coils](doc://man-heron-ignition#ignition-coils) overview.

# Repair Procedure

Replace the failed coil with IC-2044-A and inspect spark plug SP-1110 for fouling or erosion, replacing it if it is outside the gap specification. Follow PROC-IGN-COIL and the [Heron Ignition Service Manual :: Ignition Coil Replacement Procedure](doc://man-heron-ignition#ignition-coils/ignition-coil-replacement-procedure) for boot seating and torque values. Verify SP-1110 specifications against [Heron Ignition Service Manual :: Spark Plug Selection and Specifications](doc://man-heron-ignition#spark-plugs/spark-plug-selection-and-specifications) before reinstallation, then clear codes and road test to confirm P0302 and P0352 do not reset. See [Heron Ignition Service Manual :: Spark Plugs](doc://man-heron-ignition#spark-plugs) for related maintenance guidance.
