title: Lumen 2.0T electric power steering torque sensor fault causing assist loss

# Condition

Some Lumen 2.0T vehicles may exhibit a sudden or intermittent loss of power steering assist accompanied by a steering warning lamp. When the condition occurs, DTC C1556 is stored in the EPS control module. Drivers typically report heavy steering effort at low speeds, and the assist may temporarily return after an ignition cycle. For a description of normal system behavior, refer to the [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Steering System Overview](doc://man-lumen-steering#steering-system-overview).

# Cause

This condition is caused by an internal short within the torque sensor element of the electric power steering motor assembly, part number EPS-8740. The fault sets DTC C1556 when the module detects an out-of-range or implausible torque signal during the rationality check. The affected sensor circuit is integral to the motor assembly and cannot be serviced separately. Background on the affected component is provided in the [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Electric Power Steering Motor](doc://man-lumen-steering#steering-system-overview/electric-power-steering-motor), and the code itself is documented under [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: EPS Torque Sensor Fault](doc://man-lumen-steering#diagnostic-trouble-codes/eps-torque-sensor-fault).

# Diagnostic Procedure

Begin by retrieving stored codes and confirming DTC C1556 is present before proceeding. Follow PROC-EPS-DIAG to verify the torque sensor signal, harness continuity, and module supply voltage in sequence. If C1556 returns after clearing and the torque signal remains implausible with a known-good harness, the motor assembly is confirmed faulty. The full step sequence is given in the [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Electric Power Steering Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-lumen-steering#electric-power-steering-sub-system-service/electric-power-steering-diagnosis-procedure), and the related code table is in the [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Diagnostic Trouble Codes](doc://man-lumen-steering#diagnostic-trouble-codes).

# Repair Procedure

Replace the electric power steering motor assembly EPS-8740 as a complete unit; do not attempt to repair the torque sensor in isolation. After installation, perform the torque sensor calibration and clear C1556, then road test to confirm assist returns across the full speed range as outlined in PROC-EPS-DIAG. Verify no related codes remain stored in the EPS module after the repair. Component access and removal steps are covered in the [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Electric Power Steering Sub-System Service](doc://man-lumen-steering#electric-power-steering-sub-system-service).
