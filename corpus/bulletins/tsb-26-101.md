title: "Falcon 2.0T turbocharger oil-feed coking causing boost loss and underboost codes"
area: bulletins
---

# Condition

Some Falcon 2.0T vehicles may exhibit a loss of boost, reduced power under acceleration, and rough running that can set misfire code P0300. In affected units the turbocharger assembly, part number TBO-3470, exhibits restricted bearing lubrication leading to shaft drag and underboost. Drivers may report a whine, hesitation, or MIL illumination during sustained highway loads. See the [Falcon Engine Service Manual :: Forced Induction (Turbocharger)](doc://man-falcon-engine#forced-induction-turbocharger) for normal boost behavior.

# Cause

The condition is caused by oil-feed coking that restricts the lubrication passage to turbocharger TBO-3470, the result of degraded oil and a blocked or fatigued oil-feed kit, part number OFK-3420. Carbon deposits in the feed line starve the center bearing housing, increasing friction and reducing achievable boost. The [Falcon Engine Service Manual :: Lubrication and Crankcase Ventilation](doc://man-falcon-engine#lubrication-and-crankcase-ventilation) describes the oil supply circuit feeding the turbocharger.

# Diagnostic Procedure

Confirm the boost complaint and check for stored or current P0300 using the [Falcon Engine Service Manual :: Misfire Diagnosis](doc://man-falcon-engine#misfire-diagnosis), then cross-reference the related [Harrier Engine Service Manual :: Misfire Diagnostic Trouble Code Reference](doc://man-harrier-engine#diagnosis/misfire-diagnostic-trouble-code-reference) for shared code definitions. Inspect turbocharger TBO-3470 for shaft play and oil-feed restriction, and verify actual boost against commanded boost with a scan tool. Follow the [Harrier Engine Service Manual :: Misfire Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-harrier-engine#diagnosis/misfire-diagnosis-procedure) to rule out ignition and fuel contributors before condemning the turbocharger. Removing the oil-feed line and inspecting for coking confirms the diagnosis.

# Repair Procedure

Replace turbocharger TBO-3470 and the oil-feed kit OFK-3420 together following the [Falcon Engine Service Manual :: Turbocharger Replacement](doc://man-falcon-engine#forced-induction-turbocharger/turbocharger-replacement) (PROC-TURBO). Perform an engine oil and filter change per the [Falcon Engine Service Manual :: Oil and Filter Service](doc://man-falcon-engine#lubrication-and-crankcase-ventilation/oil-and-filter-service) (PROC-OIL) to clear contamination that caused the coking. Prime the new turbocharger oil feed before startup and verify boost recovery on a road test. Refer to the [Harrier Engine Service Manual :: Engine System Overview](doc://man-harrier-engine#engine-system-overview) for shared powertrain torque references.
