# Condition

Lumen drivers may report sudden or intermittent loss of power steering assist, with the steering warning lamp illuminated and noticeably heavier effort at low speed. A scan of the EPS module returns DTC C1556 (EPS Torque Sensor Fault), sometimes after a key cycle or over a thermal soak. Assist may temporarily return after restart, masking the condition. For an overview of the assist system, see the [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Steering System Overview](doc://man-lumen-steering#steering-system-overview).

# Cause

The fault originates in the torque sensor integrated with EPS assembly EPS-8740, where signal drift or an internal short causes the module to disable assist and store C1556. Because the torque sensor is not serviced separately, a confirmed sensor fault requires replacement of the EPS-8740 column assembly. Confirm the diagnosis using [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: EPS Torque Sensor Fault](doc://man-lumen-steering#diagnostic-trouble-codes/eps-torque-sensor-fault) and rule out low-voltage causes before replacing parts. The motor and sensor packaging is described in [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Electric Power Steering Motor](doc://man-lumen-steering#steering-system-overview/electric-power-steering-motor).

# Repair Procedure

Follow diagnostic procedure PROC-EPS-DIAG to validate the C1556 condition, then replace EPS-8740 and perform the steering angle and torque relearn. The full sequence is documented in the [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Electric Power Steering Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-lumen-steering#electric-power-steering-sub-system-service/electric-power-steering-diagnosis-procedure); clear C1556 and verify assist across the full lock range. Sub-system service references are collected under [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Electric Power Steering Sub-System Service](doc://man-lumen-steering#electric-power-steering-sub-system-service).
