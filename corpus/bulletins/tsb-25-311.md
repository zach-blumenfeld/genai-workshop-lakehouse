---

# Condition

Some Heron 2.5 vehicles may experience no airflow from the HVAC vents at any fan speed, accompanied by DTC B1352 (HVAC blower circuit malfunction) stored in the climate control module. The blower may be completely silent or may emit a faint hum without turning. The condition is typically constant once it appears rather than intermittent. See [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor Circuit Code](doc://man-heron-hvac#diagnostic-trouble-codes/blower-motor-circuit-code) for the B1352 definition and circuit details.

# Cause

The root cause is a mechanically seized blower motor, part BLW-7200, whose locked rotor draws excessive current and causes the control circuit to detect an out-of-range condition and set B1352. Debris ingestion past a degraded cabin air filter is a common contributor to the seizure. Refer to [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor and Cabin Air Filter](doc://man-heron-hvac#hvac-system-overview/blower-motor-and-cabin-air-filter) and the [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: HVAC System Overview](doc://man-heron-hvac#hvac-system-overview).

# Repair Procedure

Confirm B1352 and verify the blower motor BLW-7200 does not rotate when commanded before replacement. Replace the blower motor using PROC-BLOWER per [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: HVAC Blower Motor Replacement Procedure](doc://man-heron-hvac#air-distribution-sub-system-service/hvac-blower-motor-replacement-procedure), and inspect and replace the cabin air filter at the same time to prevent recurrence. Clear B1352 and verify airflow at all fan speeds. The comparable blower circuit fault diagnosis on the EV platform is documented in [Marlin EV Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor Circuit Fault Diagnosis](doc://man-marlin-ev-hvac#hvac-system-diagnosis/blower-motor-circuit-fault-diagnosis).
