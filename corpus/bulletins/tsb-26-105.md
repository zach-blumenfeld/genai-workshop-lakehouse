title: Harrier water pump seal weep causing engine over-temperature condition
area: bulletins
---

# Condition

Affected Harrier vehicles may set DTC P0217 (Engine Over-Temperature Condition) accompanied by a high-temperature warning and coolant loss over time. Drivers may notice a sweet odor, a low-coolant message, or a temperature gauge that climbs under load or at idle. A pink or dried residue trail near the pump weep hole is a common visual indicator. Diagnostic background for this code is provided in [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Thermostat and Temperature Faults](doc://man-harrier-cooling#cooling-system-diagnosis/thermostat-and-temperature-faults), and a model-specific writeup appears in [Lumen Cooling Service Manual :: P0217 Engine Over-Temperature Condition](doc://man-lumen-cooling#diagnostic-trouble-codes/p0217-engine-over-temperature-condition).

# Cause

The condition is caused by a failing water pump shaft seal that weeps coolant and eventually allows air ingestion, reducing flow and causing overheating. The original water pump, part number WP-6120, has been superseded by an improved-seal unit, part number WP-6122. Always confirm which pump is installed using [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Water Pump Identification and Supersession](doc://man-harrier-cooling#coolant-circulation/water-pump-identification-and-supersession), and review pump operation in [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Coolant Circulation](doc://man-harrier-cooling#coolant-circulation).

# Repair Procedure

Replace the water pump with the superseding part number WP-6122 following PROC-WATER-PUMP, using a new gasket or O-ring and torquing the fasteners in the specified sequence. Inspect the drive belt and tensioner while access is available, and verify the weep hole on the new pump is clear before refilling. The complete removal and installation steps are documented in [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Water Pump Replacement Procedure](doc://man-harrier-cooling#coolant-circulation/water-pump-replacement-procedure).

# Coolant Service

After pump replacement, perform a full coolant flush and refill following PROC-COOLANT, using the specified coolant type and bleeding all air from the system. Run the engine to operating temperature and verify the fans cycle and the gauge stabilizes before releasing the vehicle. Clear DTC P0217 and confirm it does not return on a road test. The fill and bleed sequence is detailed in [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Coolant Flush and Refill Procedure](doc://man-harrier-cooling#coolant-service/coolant-flush-and-refill-procedure).
