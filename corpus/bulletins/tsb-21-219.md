# Condition

Heron vehicles may exhibit an inoperative or intermittent cabin blower that fails to respond to fan speed changes, with diagnostic trouble code B1352 stored in the HVAC control module. B1352 indicates a blower motor circuit malfunction, and airflow may be absent at all speed settings while the fault is active. Customers often report no defrost or heat delivery as a secondary symptom. The affected component is described in the [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor and Cabin Air Filter](doc://man-heron-hvac#hvac-system-overview/blower-motor-and-cabin-air-filter).

# Cause

The B1352 fault is caused by a failed blower motor, part number BLW-7200, whose worn brushes create an open or high-resistance condition in the motor circuit. The control module detects the circuit fault and sets the code while disabling blower output. The code definition is documented in the [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor Circuit Code](doc://man-heron-hvac#diagnostic-trouble-codes/blower-motor-circuit-code). The diagnostic approach for the equivalent fault on the EV platform appears in the [Marlin EV Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor Circuit Fault Diagnosis](doc://man-marlin-ev-hvac#hvac-system-diagnosis/blower-motor-circuit-fault-diagnosis).

# Repair Procedure

Replace the blower motor BLW-7200 by following PROC-BLOWER as detailed in the [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: HVAC Blower Motor Replacement Procedure](doc://man-heron-hvac#air-distribution-sub-system-service/hvac-blower-motor-replacement-procedure). Inspect the cabin air filter for debris during the repair and replace it if restricted, as covered in the [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Air Distribution Sub-System Service](doc://man-heron-hvac#air-distribution-sub-system-service). After installation, verify blower operation across all speeds and clear B1352. General HVAC component locations are available in the [Heron Hvac Service Manual](doc://man-heron-hvac).
