# Condition

Affected Harrier 3.0 TD vehicles may set DTC P0217 (Engine Over-Temperature Condition) and display a high coolant temperature warning. Drivers often report a sweet coolant odor and visible coolant residue at the front of the engine before the temperature gauge climbs. The P0217 code sets when coolant temperature exceeds the calibrated overheat threshold, frequently after coolant loss. A cross-model description of this code is available in the [Lumen Cooling Service Manual :: P0217 Engine Over-Temperature Condition](doc://man-lumen-cooling#diagnostic-trouble-codes/p0217-engine-over-temperature-condition).

# Cause

The over-temperature condition is caused by a failing mechanical seal in the water pump, part number WP-6122, which allows coolant to weep past the shaft and reduces circulation. As the WP-6122 seal degrades, coolant level drops and flow falls below what is needed to control engine temperature, setting P0217. Confirm the installed pump revision before ordering, as supersession applies. Identification and supersession details are in the [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Water Pump Identification and Supersession](doc://man-harrier-cooling#coolant-circulation/water-pump-identification-and-supersession).

# Repair Procedure

Replace the water pump WP-6122 using procedure PROC-WATER-PUMP, inspecting the drive belt and pulley for coolant contamination during the repair. Observe the specified torque sequence on the pump housing fasteners to ensure the new seal mates correctly and does not weep on restart. Broader cooling-system fault context is provided in the [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Thermostat and Temperature Faults](doc://man-harrier-cooling#cooling-system-diagnosis/thermostat-and-temperature-faults). Step-by-step removal and installation are covered in the [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Water Pump Replacement Procedure](doc://man-harrier-cooling#coolant-circulation/water-pump-replacement-procedure).

# Coolant Service and Refill

After pump replacement, perform a full coolant flush and refill per procedure PROC-COOLANT to remove contaminated fluid and purge trapped air. Bleed the system following the prescribed sequence, as residual air pockets can cause a false over-temperature reading after the repair. Top off to the correct level with the specified coolant and verify temperature stabilizes during a road test. The complete fill and bleed instructions are in the [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Coolant Flush and Refill Procedure](doc://man-harrier-cooling#coolant-service/coolant-flush-and-refill-procedure).
