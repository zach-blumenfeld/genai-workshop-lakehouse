title: "Falcon 2.0T: Low generator output sets system voltage low P0562 at idle"
area: bulletins
---

# Condition

Some Falcon 2.0T vehicles may set DTC P0562 (system voltage low) and display a battery warning lamp, most noticeably at idle with electrical loads applied. Owners may report dimming lights or a slow crank as the battery, part BAT-1200, gradually discharges. The condition often improves at higher engine speeds and worsens when the alternator cannot keep up with demand. See [Falcon Charging Service Manual :: System Voltage Codes](doc://man-falcon-charging#diagnostic-trouble-codes/system-voltage-codes) for the P0562 definition.

# Cause

The underlying cause is insufficient generator output from the alternator, part ALT-8810, whose regulator allows charging voltage to fall below the threshold that sets P0562 at low engine speeds. Because the alternator cannot maintain system voltage under load, the battery supplies the deficit until it too falls below the monitored limit. Refer to [Falcon Charging Service Manual :: Alternator](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-overview/alternator) and the broader [Falcon Charging Service Manual :: Charging System Overview](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-overview).

# Diagnostic Procedure

Run charging diagnostic PROC-CHG-DIAG to measure charging voltage and ripple at idle and at 2000 rpm, following [Falcon Charging Service Manual :: Charging System Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-diagnosis-sub-system/charging-system-diagnosis-procedure). Confirm the state of battery BAT-1200 with a conductance test before condemning the alternator, since a degraded battery can mask or mimic ALT-8810 output faults. The full code reference is in [Falcon Charging Service Manual :: Diagnostic Trouble Codes](doc://man-falcon-charging#diagnostic-trouble-codes).

# Repair Procedure

If charging output is confirmed below specification, replace the alternator ALT-8810 using PROC-ALT and retest charging voltage across the load range. Verify P0562 does not reset and that battery BAT-1200 recharges to a stable resting voltage after repair. Battery replacement, if indicated by the conductance test, is covered in [Falcon Charging Service Manual :: Battery Replacement Procedure](doc://man-falcon-charging#battery-sub-system-service/battery-replacement-procedure).
