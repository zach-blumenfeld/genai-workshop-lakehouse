title: "Falcon 2.0T: Timing chain stretch sets crankshaft/camshaft correlation fault"
area: bulletins
---

# Condition

Falcon 2.0T vehicles may store diagnostic trouble code P0016 (crankshaft position/camshaft position correlation, bank 1 sensor A) and may also set P0011 (camshaft position timing over-advanced or system performance, bank 1). Symptoms include a hard or extended crank, rough cold start, reduced power, and an illuminated malfunction indicator lamp. P0016 indicates the measured crank-to-cam phase has drifted outside the correlation window. Begin diagnosis using the [Falcon Engine Service Manual :: Timing System](doc://man-falcon-engine#timing-system).

# Cause

The primary cause is mechanical stretch of the timing chain TC-3340, which retards camshaft timing relative to the crankshaft and sets P0016. As the chain elongates beyond its wear limit, the variable valve timing actuator VVT-3360 can no longer achieve commanded phase, which may additionally set P0011. Inspect the chain guides and tensioner condition before condemning related actuators. Phasing operation is described in the [Falcon Engine Service Manual :: Variable Valve Timing System](doc://man-falcon-engine#variable-valve-timing-system).

# Repair Procedure

Perform PROC-CPS-CMP to verify crankshaft and camshaft position alignment, then replace the stretched timing chain TC-3340 along with its guides and tensioner per the published service procedure. If the VVT-3360 solenoid fails to hold commanded position after the chain is renewed, replace it as well. Chain renewal steps are in the [Falcon Engine Service Manual :: Timing Chain Service](doc://man-falcon-engine#timing-system/timing-chain-service), and solenoid replacement is in the [Falcon Engine Service Manual :: VVT Solenoid Replacement](doc://man-falcon-engine#variable-valve-timing-system/vvt-solenoid-replacement). The closely related Harrier platform shares this architecture; equivalent steps are in the [Harrier Engine Service Manual :: Timing Chain Service](doc://man-harrier-engine#timing-sub-system/timing-chain-service) and the [Harrier Engine Service Manual :: Variable Valve Timing Solenoid Replacement](doc://man-harrier-engine#timing-sub-system/variable-valve-timing-solenoid-replacement). After repair, clear P0016 and P0011 and confirm the codes do not return after several drive cycles.
