title: "Heron 2.5: Blower motor inoperative from open blower circuit"

# Condition

Some 2.5L Heron vehicles may exhibit a blower motor that is inoperative at all fan speeds, leaving the cabin with no airflow from the HVAC system. DTC B1352 (Blower Motor Circuit) sets as a current or stored fault when the control module detects an open in the blower circuit. Customers may report no heat or air conditioning delivery even though temperature control responds normally. Refer to [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor Circuit Code](doc://man-heron-hvac#diagnostic-trouble-codes/blower-motor-circuit-code) for the criteria that set B1352.

# Cause

DTC B1352 on these vehicles is most often caused by a failed blower motor BLW-7200, an open in the blower supply or ground circuit, or a faulty blower control connector. A seized motor or worn brushes can also create an open-circuit condition that sets the code. Review blower motor operation in [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor and Cabin Air Filter](doc://man-heron-hvac#hvac-system-overview/blower-motor-and-cabin-air-filter) and the overall system in [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: HVAC System Overview](doc://man-heron-hvac#hvac-system-overview) before condemning BLW-7200.

# Repair Procedure

Confirm the open circuit, then replace the blower motor BLW-7200 following procedure PROC-BLOWER, inspecting the connector for heat damage or backed-out terminals during removal. After installation, verify operation at all fan speeds and confirm DTC B1352 does not return. Replace the cabin air filter if it is restricted, as added load can shorten motor life. Detailed steps are provided in [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: HVAC Blower Motor Replacement Procedure](doc://man-heron-hvac#air-distribution-sub-system-service/hvac-blower-motor-replacement-procedure), with related service grouped under [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Air Distribution Sub-System Service](doc://man-heron-hvac#air-distribution-sub-system-service).
