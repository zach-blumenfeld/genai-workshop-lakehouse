# Condition

Certain Heron vehicles may exhibit no cabin airflow from the HVAC vents at any blower speed setting, accompanied by DTC B1352 (Blower Motor Circuit Malfunction). The customer may report that the fan does not respond to the speed selector and that defrost and climate functions are ineffective. Confirm the concern by retrieving B1352 from the HVAC control module and verifying the blower does not operate across the commanded speed range. See [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor Circuit Code](doc://man-heron-hvac#diagnostic-trouble-codes/blower-motor-circuit-code) for the diagnostic criteria tied to this code.

# Cause

The condition is caused by an internal open in blower motor BLW-7200, which interrupts the motor circuit and prevents the control module from driving the fan. Carbon buildup and brush wear within BLW-7200 increase resistance until the circuit faults and B1352 sets. Refer to [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor and Cabin Air Filter](doc://man-heron-hvac#hvac-system-overview/blower-motor-and-cabin-air-filter) for component location and the [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: HVAC System Overview](doc://man-heron-hvac#hvac-system-overview) for circuit context.

# Repair Procedure

Begin by confirming the fault is internal to the motor and not a wiring or module concern; cross-reference the diagnostic approach in [Marlin EV Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor Circuit Fault Diagnosis](doc://man-marlin-ev-hvac#hvac-system-diagnosis/blower-motor-circuit-fault-diagnosis) where the circuit topology is similar. Replace blower motor BLW-7200 following procedure PROC-BLOWER, taking care to seat the connector fully and verify the cabin air filter is unobstructed. Detailed removal and installation steps are provided in [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: HVAC Blower Motor Replacement Procedure](doc://man-heron-hvac#air-distribution-sub-system-service/hvac-blower-motor-replacement-procedure). After installing BLW-7200, clear B1352 and confirm airflow at all blower speeds.
