# Condition

Summit 5.0 V8 vehicles may store DTC C0040, indicating a right front wheel speed sensor circuit fault, with the ABS and traction control warning lamps illuminated. The condition may be intermittent and most often appears after the vehicle encounters road salt or moisture intrusion at the sensor connector. Normal service braking is retained, but ABS and stability functions are disabled while the code is active. The code definition is listed in the [Summit Abs Service Manual :: Wheel Speed Sensor Circuit Codes](doc://man-summit-abs#diagnostic-trouble-codes/wheel-speed-sensor-circuit-codes).

# Cause

C0040 is most commonly caused by corrosion or an open in the circuit feeding wheel speed sensor WSS-3300 at the right front corner. Water wicking into the connector raises circuit resistance until the EBCM can no longer read a valid signal. Sensor operation and tone-ring interaction are described in the [Summit Abs Service Manual :: Wheel Speed Sensors](doc://man-summit-abs#abs-system-overview/wheel-speed-sensors) section. A full list of related codes is available under [Summit Abs Service Manual :: Diagnostic Trouble Codes](doc://man-summit-abs#diagnostic-trouble-codes).

# Diagnostic and Repair Procedure

Begin with procedure PROC-ABS-DIAG to confirm the fault and isolate it to the sensor circuit versus the module, using the [Summit Abs Service Manual :: ABS Module Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-summit-abs#ebcm-and-hydraulic-sub-system-service/abs-module-diagnosis-procedure). If the circuit is confirmed open or high-resistance, replace sensor WSS-3300 following procedure PROC-WSS as detailed in the [Summit Abs Service Manual :: Wheel Speed Sensor Replacement Procedure](doc://man-summit-abs#wheel-speed-sensor-sub-system-service/wheel-speed-sensor-replacement-procedure). Clear C0040, verify a valid wheel speed signal during a road test, and confirm the ABS lamp extinguishes. General system context is provided in the [Summit Abs Service Manual :: ABS System Overview](doc://man-summit-abs#abs-system-overview).
