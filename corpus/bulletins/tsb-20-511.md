title: "Falcon 2.0T: System voltage low from failed 180A alternator"
area: bulletins
---

# Condition

Some 2.0T Falcon vehicles may exhibit a battery warning lamp, dimming lights, or a no-start condition accompanied by DTC P0562 (System Voltage Low) and DTC P0620 (Generator Control Circuit). Drivers may report stalling under high electrical load or repeated dead-battery complaints. Both codes may be stored together when charging output falls below the regulated target. See [Falcon Charging Service Manual :: System Voltage Codes](doc://man-falcon-charging#diagnostic-trouble-codes/system-voltage-codes) for the conditions that set P0562.

# Cause

DTC P0562 and DTC P0620 on these vehicles are most often caused by a failed 180A alternator ALT-8810 with worn brushes or an internal regulator fault that interrupts the generator control circuit. A loose or corroded B+ output connection can produce the same low-voltage symptoms. Review charging system operation in [Falcon Charging Service Manual :: Alternator](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-overview/alternator) and the control circuit description in [Falcon Charging Service Manual :: Generator Control Circuit Code](doc://man-falcon-charging#diagnostic-trouble-codes/generator-control-circuit-code) before condemning ALT-8810.

# Diagnosis

Perform charging system test PROC-CHG-DIAG to measure regulated output voltage and ripple under load before replacing the alternator. Confirm battery state of charge, inspect the B+ cable and ground, and verify the generator control circuit communicates correctly with the ECM. If output remains below specification with P0562 and P0620 current, the alternator ALT-8810 is the confirmed failure. The full test sequence appears in [Falcon Charging Service Manual :: Charging System Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-diagnosis-sub-system/charging-system-diagnosis-procedure).

# Repair Procedure

Replace the 180A alternator ALT-8810 following procedure PROC-ALT, taking care to disconnect the battery before servicing the B+ terminal. After installation, verify regulated charging voltage and confirm DTC P0562 and P0620 do not return after a road test. Inspect and retorque the output cable to specification. Detailed removal and installation steps are provided in [Falcon Charging Service Manual :: Alternator Replacement Procedure](doc://man-falcon-charging#alternator-sub-system-service/alternator-replacement-procedure), with supporting overview information in [Falcon Charging Service Manual :: Charging System Overview](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-overview).
