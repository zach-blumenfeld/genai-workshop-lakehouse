# Condition

Heron 2.5 owners may report a knocking or clunking noise from the front of the vehicle over bumps, accompanied by uneven or feathered tire wear. The condition is most often attributable to a worn front strut assembly STRT-8500 that no longer controls suspension travel adequately. Review the component description in the [Heron Suspension Service Manual :: Front Strut Sub-System](doc://man-heron-suspension#front-strut-sub-system) before condemning the part. The cross-platform [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Front Strut Assembly](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#suspension-system-overview/front-strut-assembly) offers additional context on strut construction.

# Cause

Internal damping degradation within strut assembly STRT-8500 allows excess vertical movement, producing the knock under load and accelerating irregular tread wear. Worn mounts and a fatigued spring seat compound the noise as mileage accumulates. The [Heron Suspension Service Manual](doc://man-heron-suspension) details inspection criteria, and the [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Suspension System Overview](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#suspension-system-overview) explains how a failing STRT-8500 transmits forces into the chassis.

# Repair Procedure

Replace front strut assembly STRT-8500 on the affected side, or in axle sets when wear is comparable, following PROC-STRUT as documented in the [Heron Suspension Service Manual :: Front Strut Replacement](doc://man-heron-suspension#front-strut-sub-system/front-strut-replacement). The equivalent steps appear in the [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Front Strut Replacement Procedure](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#front-strut-sub-system-service/front-strut-replacement-procedure) for technicians cross-trained on that platform. After installing the new STRT-8500, perform a four-wheel alignment per PROC-ALIGN using the [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Four-Wheel Alignment Procedure](doc://man-lumen-steering#wheel-alignment-sub-system-service/four-wheel-alignment-procedure). Road test to confirm the knock is eliminated before returning the vehicle.
