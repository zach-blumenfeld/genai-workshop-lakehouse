# Condition

Some Osprey 3.5 V6 vehicles may set DTC P0741 (Torque Converter Clutch Circuit Performance or Stuck Off) with reduced fuel economy and elevated engine RPM at highway cruise. Drivers may notice a flare or slip when the transmission should be in lockup, along with increased heat in the transmission fluid. The malfunction indicator lamp will typically illuminate after the code matures. Consult the [Diagnostic Trouble Code Reference](doc://man-osprey-transmission#diagnosis/diagnostic-trouble-code-reference) for the P0741 set criteria.

# Cause

P0741 is most commonly caused by a faulty shift solenoid pack TSOL-9520 that fails to apply the torque converter clutch, leaving it stuck off even when commanded on. Internal wear or contamination in TSOL-9520 can also cause erratic hydraulic control of the clutch apply circuit. Use the [Torque Converter Clutch Diagnosis](doc://man-osprey-transmission#shift-control-sub-system/torque-converter-clutch-diagnosis) routine together with [Fluid Pressure Diagnosis](doc://man-osprey-transmission#diagnosis/fluid-pressure-diagnosis) to confirm the source before replacement.

# Repair Procedure

Follow PROC-SHIFT-SOL to verify clutch apply commands and isolate the shift solenoid pack TSOL-9520 before any teardown. Complete the [Shift Solenoid Pack Replacement Procedure](doc://man-osprey-transmission#shift-control-sub-system/shift-solenoid-pack-replacement-procedure) and recheck lockup operation on a road test. Supporting diagnostic steps are found under [Diagnosis](doc://man-osprey-transmission#diagnosis) and the [Shift Control Sub-System](doc://man-osprey-transmission#shift-control-sub-system), with system background in the [Transmission System Overview](doc://man-osprey-transmission#transmission-system-overview) of the [Osprey Transmission Service Manual](doc://man-osprey-transmission).
