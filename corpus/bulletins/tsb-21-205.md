# Condition

Affected Harrier vehicles may store DTC P0217 (engine over-temperature condition) and illuminate the temperature warning lamp during sustained highway or towing loads. Drivers typically report rising coolant temperature followed by reduced engine power as the protection strategy engages. The condition is intermittent at first and worsens as coolant volume is gradually lost. Refer to [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Thermostat and Temperature Faults](doc://man-harrier-cooling#cooling-system-diagnosis/thermostat-and-temperature-faults) to rule out thermostat-related causes before proceeding.

# Cause

The root cause of P0217 in these vehicles is a weeping mechanical seal in water pump WP-6122, which allows slow coolant loss and degrades circulation. As coolant level drops, flow through the system becomes insufficient and engine temperature climbs into the over-temperature range. In some cases the resulting heat cycling also accelerates aging of the upper radiator hose RHOSE-6180, which can compound the loss. See [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Water Pump Identification and Supersession](doc://man-harrier-cooling#coolant-circulation/water-pump-identification-and-supersession) to confirm the installed pump part number, and [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Coolant Circulation](doc://man-harrier-cooling#coolant-circulation) for the circulation path background.

# Repair Procedure

Replace water pump WP-6122 following PROC-WATER-PUMP, then inspect and replace upper radiator hose RHOSE-6180 if any swelling or seepage is present. Refill and bleed the cooling system per the manual, then road-test to confirm P0217 does not return under load. Detailed torque and bleed steps are provided in [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Water Pump Replacement Procedure](doc://man-harrier-cooling#coolant-circulation/water-pump-replacement-procedure), and hose service is covered in [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Radiator Hose Replacement](doc://man-harrier-cooling#heat-rejection/radiator-hose-replacement). For broader engine context during the repair, consult the [Harrier Engine Service Manual](doc://man-harrier-engine).
