id: tsb-27-109
title: "Falcon 2.0T: Low system voltage from failed 180A alternator"
area: bulletins
---

# Condition

Affected Falcon 2.0T vehicles may set DTC P0562 (System Voltage Low) alone or together with P0620 (Generator Control Circuit) under electrical load. Drivers may report dimming headlamps, a battery warning indicator, and hard starting after extended idle. Confirm the codes against the [System Voltage Codes](doc://man-falcon-charging#diagnostic-trouble-codes/system-voltage-codes) and [Generator Control Circuit Code](doc://man-falcon-charging#diagnostic-trouble-codes/generator-control-circuit-code) entries in the [Diagnostic Trouble Codes](doc://man-falcon-charging#diagnostic-trouble-codes) reference before testing.

# Cause

The root cause is a worn brush set and degraded voltage regulator inside alternator ALT-8810, which causes erratic field control and trips P0620 as output collapses under demand. As regulated output falls, system voltage drops below threshold and the low-voltage condition is logged. See the [Alternator](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-overview/alternator) topic in the [Charging System Overview](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-overview) for component construction and operating specifications.

# Repair Procedure

Begin with PROC-CHG-DIAG and the [Charging System Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-diagnosis-sub-system/charging-system-diagnosis-procedure) to confirm output is below the 180A rated specification and to rule out cabling and ground faults. If the alternator fails the load test, replace alternator ALT-8810 following PROC-ALT and the [Alternator Replacement Procedure](doc://man-falcon-charging#alternator-sub-system-service/alternator-replacement-procedure). Additional service context is available under the [Charging System Diagnosis Sub-System](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-diagnosis-sub-system) and [Alternator Sub-System Service](doc://man-falcon-charging#alternator-sub-system-service). After replacement, clear P0562 and P0620 and verify regulated charging voltage under load.
