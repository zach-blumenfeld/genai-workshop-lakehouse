# Condition

Some Vega 2.4 vehicles may store DTC U0140 (Lost Communication with Body Control Module) in one or more controllers. When U0140 is active, operators report intermittent loss of interior lighting, power door locks, and dashboard warning telltales. The fault frequently sets after the vehicle has been driven over rough roads or following a battery disconnect. Refer to the [Network Communication Diagnostic Codes](doc://man-vega-electrical#network-communication-diagnostic-codes) section for the full description of U0140 and its companion codes.

# Cause

DTC U0140 is most commonly caused by a degraded CAN bus connection at the body control module BCM-9920, where corrosion or a loose terminal interrupts serial data between the BCM and other modules. In rarer cases the BCM-9920 itself loses its internal communication driver and must be reconfigured or replaced. See [Lost Communication with BCM and Cluster](doc://man-vega-electrical#network-communication-diagnostic-codes/lost-communication-with-bcm-and-cluster) for the diagnostic decision tree, and the [Body Control Module](doc://man-vega-electrical#electrical-system-overview/body-control-module) overview for the location and pinout of BCM-9920.

# Repair Procedure

Follow PROC-COMMS-DIAG to confirm U0140 and isolate the open or high-resistance segment of the CAN bus before replacing any part. Begin with the [CAN Bus Communication Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-vega-electrical#network-and-harness-sub-system-service/can-bus-communication-diagnosis-procedure) to measure bus resistance and inspect the BCM-9920 connector. If the BCM-9920 is replaced, complete the [Body Control Module Configuration Procedure](doc://man-vega-electrical#bcm-sub-system-service/body-control-module-configuration-procedure) so the new module is programmed to the vehicle. General wiring and module access information is found in the [Vega Electrical Service Manual](doc://man-vega-electrical) and its [Electrical System Overview](doc://man-vega-electrical#electrical-system-overview).
