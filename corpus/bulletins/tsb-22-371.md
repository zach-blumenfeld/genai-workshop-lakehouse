# Condition

Some Falcon 1.6 vehicles may set DTC P0562 (System Voltage Low) with an illuminated charging warning indicator. Drivers typically notice dimming headlamps, slow cranking, and a battery telltale that appears under electrical load. The condition often progresses to a no-start if left unaddressed. The [System Voltage Codes](doc://man-falcon-charging#diagnostic-trouble-codes/system-voltage-codes) section describes the P0562 set criteria in detail.

# Cause

P0562 is most often caused by a failing alternator ALT-8810 that can no longer maintain charging output above the regulated threshold, which in turn leaves the battery BAT-1200 undercharged. A weak or sulfated BAT-1200 can also pull system voltage low even when ALT-8810 output is marginal. Review the [Alternator](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-overview/alternator) and [Battery](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-overview/battery) descriptions, and confirm the code against the [Diagnostic Trouble Codes](doc://man-falcon-charging#diagnostic-trouble-codes) reference.

# Repair Procedure

Follow PROC-ALT to verify charging output before condemning the alternator ALT-8810. Begin with the [Charging System Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-diagnosis-sub-system/charging-system-diagnosis-procedure) to load-test the system and measure regulated voltage. If BAT-1200 fails its capacity test, complete the [Battery Replacement Procedure](doc://man-falcon-charging#battery-sub-system-service/battery-replacement-procedure) and recheck output. Full system context is available in the [Charging System Overview](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-overview) within the [Falcon Charging Service Manual](doc://man-falcon-charging).
