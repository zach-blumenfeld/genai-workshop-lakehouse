title: "Osprey 3.5 V6: High-pressure fuel pump wear sets low fuel rail pressure P0087"
area: bulletins
---

# Condition

Some Osprey 3.5 V6 vehicles may exhibit extended cranking, a stumble under hard acceleration, and an illuminated malfunction indicator. The PCM will store DTC P0087 "Fuel Rail/System Pressure - Too Low" when commanded rail pressure cannot be maintained under demand. Customers most often report the concern during wide-open-throttle or trailer-towing conditions when fuel demand is highest. Refer to [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Pressure and Sensor Faults](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-system-diagnosis/fuel-pressure-and-sensor-faults) for the conditions under which P0087 sets.

# Cause

This condition is caused by mechanical wear of the high-pressure GDI fuel pump, part number HPFP-4120, whose internal cam follower and plunger degrade and reduce delivered rail pressure over time. As HPFP-4120 wears, it can no longer meet the PCM pressure target, so the controller sets P0087. Pump construction and operating-pressure details are documented in [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: High-Pressure Fuel Pump (GDI)](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery/high-pressure-fuel-pump-gdi).

# Diagnosis

Run PROC-FUEL-DIAG to compare commanded versus actual rail pressure and to verify low-pressure supply before condemning the high-pressure pump. Confirm that the fuel rail pressure sensor FRS-4180 is reporting accurately, since a skewed FRS-4180 signal can mimic P0087 without any actual pressure loss. The complete diagnostic sequence is provided in [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Trim and Pressure Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-system-diagnosis/fuel-trim-and-pressure-diagnosis-procedure) and the broader [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel System Diagnosis](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-system-diagnosis) section.

# Repair Procedure

If supply pressure is normal and FRS-4180 reads correctly but actual rail pressure remains below target, replace the high-pressure pump HPFP-4120. Perform PROC-FUEL-PUMP and follow the [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Pump Replacement Procedure](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery/fuel-pump-replacement-procedure), observing all fuel-system depressurization and cleanliness requirements. After installing HPFP-4120, prime the system, clear codes, and verify P0087 does not return under load; see [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Delivery](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery) and the master [Osprey Fuel Service Manual](doc://man-osprey-fuel) for torque and bleed specifications.
