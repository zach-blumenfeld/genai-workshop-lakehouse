title: Falcon 2.0T crank/cam correlation P0016 due to timing belt tensioner

# Condition

Falcon 2.0T vehicles may store diagnostic trouble code P0016 (Crankshaft Position - Camshaft Position Correlation, Bank 1 Sensor A) accompanied by a hard start, rough idle, or reduced power complaint. The malfunction indicator lamp will illuminate and the engine control module may limit timing advance as a protective measure. Confirm the P0016 fault is current and inspect for related correlation codes before disassembly. General engine architecture and sensor locations are described in the [Falcon Engine Service Manual](doc://man-falcon-engine).

# Cause

This condition is caused by wear and tension loss in the automatic timing belt tensioner assembly, part number TB-3322, which allows the belt to skip a tooth and shift the camshaft timing relative to the crankshaft. As the TB-3322 hydraulic damper loses preload, belt slack under cold start torque triggers the P0016 correlation fault. The detailed inspection criteria for the tensioner are covered in the [Falcon Engine Service Manual :: Timing System](doc://man-falcon-engine#timing-system) chapter. A comparable failure mode and identification guidance is documented in the [Harrier Engine Service Manual :: Timing Sub-System](doc://man-harrier-engine#timing-sub-system).

# Repair Procedure

Replace the timing belt tensioner TB-3322 together with the timing belt as a set, following procedure PROC-TIMING-BELT, and verify all timing marks align before rotating the engine by hand two full revolutions. After installation, clear P0016 and confirm correlation is within specification on a road test. The full procedure is provided in the [Falcon Engine Service Manual :: Timing Belt and Tensioner Service](doc://man-falcon-engine#timing-system/timing-belt-and-tensioner-service) section. For cross-platform reference, equivalent steps appear in the [Harrier Engine Service Manual :: Timing Belt and Tensioner Replacement](doc://man-harrier-engine#timing-sub-system/timing-belt-and-tensioner-replacement) and belt specifications in the [Lumen Engine Service Manual :: Timing Belt Specifications and Identification](doc://man-lumen-engine#valve-timing-sub-system/timing-belt-specifications-and-identification).
