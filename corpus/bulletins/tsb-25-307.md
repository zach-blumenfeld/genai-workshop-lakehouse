# Condition

Ridgeline HD 6.7 TD vehicles may exhibit a sagging load bed, excessive rebound, and a harsh ride over uneven surfaces, particularly when loaded. The symptom develops gradually as the rear damper loses control of body motion. The affected component is rear shock absorber SHK-8520. System orientation is provided in the [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Suspension System Overview](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#suspension-system-overview).

# Cause

The condition is caused by internal seal wear and fluid loss in rear shock absorber SHK-8520, which reduces damping force and allows the rear axle to oscillate. Vehicles operated under sustained heavy loads or in high-temperature duty cycles are more likely to exhibit accelerated wear. A leaking SHK-8520 will often show oil film on the shock body. Component detail is documented in the [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Rear Shock Absorber](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#suspension-system-overview/rear-shock-absorber) section.

# Repair Procedure

Replace the affected rear shock absorber SHK-8520 in axle-pair sets following the [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Rear Shock Sub-System Service](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#rear-shock-sub-system-service). If the inspection reveals related lower control arm wear, complete procedure PROC-CONTROL-ARM as described in the [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Control Arm Replacement Procedure](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#control-arm-and-sway-bar-sub-system-service/control-arm-replacement-procedure). For prior guidance on control-arm-related front clunk and tire wear, see [Ridgeline HD: Front clunk and uneven tire wear from worn lower control arm](doc://tsb-20-508). After replacement, confirm correct ride height and verify ride quality on a loaded road test.
