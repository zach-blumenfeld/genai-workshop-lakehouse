# Condition

Affected Ridgeline HD vehicles may exhibit a knocking or clunking noise from the front suspension over bumps and during low-speed maneuvering. The noise originates from front strut assembly STRT-8500 and is most noticeable on rough or uneven surfaces. In some cases drivers also report a slight degradation in ride quality and steering feel. See [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Front Strut Assembly](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#suspension-system-overview/front-strut-assembly) to confirm the affected component.

# Cause

The noise is caused by internal wear within front strut assembly STRT-8500, which develops play in the damper and upper mount over high mileage and heavy-load use. The resulting looseness produces the characteristic knock as the suspension cycles. General suspension context is provided in [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Suspension System Overview](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#suspension-system-overview). A related condition affecting the same corner is described in [Ridgeline HD: Front clunk and uneven tire wear from worn lower control arm](doc://tsb-20-508).

# Repair Procedure

Replace front strut assembly STRT-8500 in axle pairs following PROC-STRUT, then perform a four-wheel alignment before returning the vehicle. Detailed spring-compression and torque steps are provided in [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Front Strut Replacement Procedure](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#front-strut-sub-system-service/front-strut-replacement-procedure) and [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Front Strut Sub-System Service](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#front-strut-sub-system-service). For a comparable platform procedure, see [Heron Suspension Service Manual :: Front Strut Replacement](doc://man-heron-suspension#front-strut-sub-system/front-strut-replacement). A later related bulletin is [Ridgeline HD 6.7 TD: Front clunk and uneven ride from worn front strut](doc://tsb-22-350).
