title: Osprey 3.5 V6 high-pressure fuel pump wear causing low rail pressure under load
area: bulletins
---

# Condition

Affected Osprey 3.5 V6 vehicles may set DTC P0087 (Fuel Rail/System Pressure Too Low) during hard acceleration or sustained high-load operation. Drivers report hesitation, reduced power, and an illuminated MIL when the rail cannot maintain commanded pressure. Freeze-frame data typically shows a large negative deviation between commanded and actual rail pressure at high engine load. See [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Pressure and Sensor Faults](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-system-diagnosis/fuel-pressure-and-sensor-faults) for the full description of this code.

# Cause

The condition is caused by accelerated wear of the high-pressure fuel pump cam follower and plunger in part number HPFP-4120, which reduces the pump's ability to sustain rail pressure under load. As the HPFP-4120 internal components wear, peak delivery falls off while idle and light-load pressure remain normal, masking the fault until high demand. Pump construction and operating theory are described in [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: High-Pressure Fuel Pump (GDI)](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery/high-pressure-fuel-pump-gdi), within the broader [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Delivery](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery) section.

# Diagnostic Verification

Before replacing any component, verify the fault following PROC-FUEL-DIAG by commanding a pressure sweep and comparing actual rail pressure against the fuel rail pressure sensor, part number FRS-4180. If the FRS-4180 reads accurately but actual pressure falls short under load, the pump is confirmed as the failed component rather than the sensor. The full verification routine is documented in [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Trim and Pressure Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-system-diagnosis/fuel-trim-and-pressure-diagnosis-procedure), and sensor replacement, if the FRS-4180 is also out of range, is covered in [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Rail Pressure Sensor Replacement](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-rail-and-pressure-sensing/fuel-rail-pressure-sensor-replacement).

# Repair Procedure

Replace the high-pressure fuel pump HPFP-4120 following PROC-FUEL-PUMP, observing all fuel-system depressurization and cleanliness requirements. Install a new cam follower with the pump and torque the mounting fasteners in sequence to specification. After installation, prime the system, clear DTC P0087, and confirm rail pressure holds under a high-load verification drive. The complete procedure is in [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Pump Replacement Procedure](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery/fuel-pump-replacement-procedure).
