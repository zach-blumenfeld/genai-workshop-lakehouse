title: "Vega 2.4: Lost communication with body control module on CAN bus"
area: bulletins
---

# Condition

Some 2.4L Vega vehicles may exhibit intermittent or total loss of communication between the body control module and other modules on the high-speed CAN bus. When this occurs, DTC U0140 (Lost Communication With Body Control Module) sets as a current or stored fault, often accompanied by inoperative interior lighting, gauges, and power accessory functions. The condition may be temperature- or vibration-sensitive, making it difficult to reproduce on a stationary vehicle. Refer to the [Vega Electrical Service Manual :: Network Communication Diagnostic Codes](doc://man-vega-electrical#network-communication-diagnostic-codes) for the full list of related communication codes.

# Cause

DTC U0140 is most commonly caused by an internal fault within the body control module BCM-9920, an open or high-resistance connection at the BCM connector, or a damaged CAN bus circuit between the BCM and the gateway. Corrosion at the module ground or a chafed harness can also intermittently interrupt communication. Review the operation described in [Vega Electrical Service Manual :: Body Control Module](doc://man-vega-electrical#electrical-system-overview/body-control-module) and the symptom-specific diagnostic path in [Vega Electrical Service Manual :: Lost Communication with BCM and Cluster](doc://man-vega-electrical#network-communication-diagnostic-codes/lost-communication-with-bcm-and-cluster) before condemning BCM-9920.

# Diagnosis

Before replacing any module, perform the published bus integrity test PROC-COMMS-DIAG to confirm whether the fault lies in the network wiring or within the body control module itself. Measure CAN-High and CAN-Low resistance and verify proper termination, then check for power and ground at the BCM-9920 connector. If the bus checks within specification but U0140 remains, the module is the likely failure. The complete procedure is documented in [Vega Electrical Service Manual :: CAN Bus Communication Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-vega-electrical#network-and-harness-sub-system-service/can-bus-communication-diagnosis-procedure).

# Repair Procedure

If diagnosis isolates the fault to the module, replace body control module BCM-9920 and perform the configuration routine PROC-BCM-CONFIG to program the new module to the vehicle. Failure to complete configuration will leave the BCM-9920 unable to learn vehicle options and may cause U0140 to reset. Verify all accessory functions and confirm no communication codes return after a road test. Follow the detailed steps in [Vega Electrical Service Manual :: Body Control Module Configuration Procedure](doc://man-vega-electrical#bcm-sub-system-service/body-control-module-configuration-procedure) and reference the broader [Vega Electrical Service Manual :: BCM Sub-System Service](doc://man-vega-electrical#bcm-sub-system-service) section as needed.
