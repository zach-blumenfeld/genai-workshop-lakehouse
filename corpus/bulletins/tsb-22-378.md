# Condition

Some Heron 2.5 vehicles may store DTC B1352 (Blower Motor Circuit Fault) accompanied by intermittent or complete loss of cabin airflow. Operators report that the blower cuts out at certain fan speeds or stops entirely when the vehicle hits a bump. The fault may be intermittent and not always present at the time of inspection. See the [Blower Motor Circuit Code](doc://man-heron-hvac#diagnostic-trouble-codes/blower-motor-circuit-code) section for the B1352 set conditions.

# Cause

DTC B1352 is generally caused by an open or high-resistance circuit at the blower motor BLW-7200, often originating from a worn brush set or a corroded connector at the motor terminal. In some cases the BLW-7200 draws excessive current and trips the circuit protection, producing the intermittent behavior. The [Blower Motor and Cabin Air Filter](doc://man-heron-hvac#hvac-system-overview/blower-motor-and-cabin-air-filter) overview shows the location of BLW-7200, and the [Diagnostic Trouble Codes](doc://man-heron-hvac#diagnostic-trouble-codes) section lists related faults.

# Repair Procedure

Follow PROC-BLOWER to test the blower motor BLW-7200 circuit for voltage drop and current draw before replacement. If the motor fails, complete the [HVAC Blower Motor Replacement Procedure](doc://man-heron-hvac#air-distribution-sub-system-service/hvac-blower-motor-replacement-procedure) and verify operation across all fan speeds. Broader airflow component information is found in the [Air Distribution Sub-System Service](doc://man-heron-hvac#air-distribution-sub-system-service) and the [HVAC System Overview](doc://man-heron-hvac#hvac-system-overview) of the [Heron Hvac Service Manual](doc://man-heron-hvac).
