title: Falcon 2.0T timing belt tensioner may fail causing engine stall
area: recalls

# Defect Description

On affected Falcon 2.0T vehicles, the timing belt tensioner (part TB-3320) can lose hydraulic damping force, allowing the timing belt to develop slack and skip teeth on the camshaft sprocket. When belt slack alters valve timing, the engine control module sets camshaft position correlation code DTC P0016 and, in cases of failed intake timing actuation, DTC P0011, after which the engine may stall and fail to restart. The mechanical relationship between the tensioner and belt is documented in the [Falcon Engine Service Manual :: Timing Belt and Tensioner Service](doc://man-falcon-engine#timing-system/timing-belt-and-tensioner-service), part of the broader [Falcon Engine Service Manual :: Timing System](doc://man-falcon-engine#timing-system). Technicians can cross-reference the comparable timing architecture in the [Harrier Engine Service Manual :: Timing Sub-System](doc://man-harrier-engine#timing-sub-system) when confirming the fault.

# Remedy

Dealers will replace the timing belt tensioner (TB-3320) with a revised tensioner (part TB-3322) that maintains hydraulic damping over the full service life, and will replace the timing belt as part of the same operation. The repair must be performed following the documented procedure PROC-TIMING-BELT and the step-by-step guidance in the [Harrier Engine Service Manual :: Timing Belt and Tensioner Replacement](doc://man-harrier-engine#timing-sub-system/timing-belt-and-tensioner-replacement), with belt specification confirmed against the [Lumen Engine Service Manual :: Timing Belt Specifications and Identification](doc://man-lumen-engine#valve-timing-sub-system/timing-belt-specifications-and-identification). After installation, technicians clear DTC P0016 and DTC P0011 and verify cam timing alignment per the [Falcon Engine Service Manual :: Timing Belt and Tensioner Service](doc://man-falcon-engine#timing-system/timing-belt-and-tensioner-service). This repair is performed at no charge to the owner; owners can confirm eligibility by entering their VIN at https://www.nhtsa.gov/recalls.
