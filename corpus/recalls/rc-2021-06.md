# Defect Description

Affected vehicles may contain a brake master cylinder assembly, part number BMC-7780, with an internally degraded primary piston seal. Under repeated heavy braking the seal can allow hydraulic fluid to bypass internally, producing a gradual loss of pedal firmness and, in severe cases, a complete loss of front circuit pressure. Technicians investigating the condition should review the master cylinder operation described in the [Marlin EV Brakes Service Manual :: Brake Master Cylinder Service](doc://man-marlin-ev-brakes#hydraulic-actuation/brake-master-cylinder-service) and the broader [Marlin EV Brakes Service Manual :: Hydraulic Actuation](doc://man-marlin-ev-brakes#hydraulic-actuation) section. The defect is internal and may not produce a visible external leak, so a pedal-feel symptom should not be dismissed even when no fluid loss is observed.

# Remedy

Dealers must replace the affected brake master cylinder BMC-7780 with a revised unit incorporating an upgraded seal material, following the removal and installation steps in the [Marlin EV Brakes Service Manual :: Brake Master Cylinder Service](doc://man-marlin-ev-brakes#hydraulic-actuation/brake-master-cylinder-service). After replacement the system must be refilled with fresh brake fluid BF-7790 and the hydraulic circuit fully purged of air using the [Marlin EV Brakes Service Manual :: Brake Fluid Flush and Bleed Procedure](doc://man-marlin-ev-brakes#hydraulic-lines-and-fluid/brake-fluid-flush-and-bleed-procedure). Inspect the connected brake lines BR-7731 for any contamination or weeping at the unions as covered in [Marlin EV Brakes Service Manual :: Hydraulic Lines and Fluid](doc://man-marlin-ev-brakes#hydraulic-lines-and-fluid). The complete repair is performed at no charge to the owner, and a road test confirming firm pedal travel is required before the vehicle is released. Refer to the [Marlin EV Brakes Service Manual](doc://man-marlin-ev-brakes) for torque values and bleed sequence references.
