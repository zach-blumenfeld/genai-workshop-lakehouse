title: Safety Recall - ABS Pump Motor Failure Loss of Anti-Lock Braking

# Defect Description

Affected vehicles are equipped with an electronic brake control module assembly, part number EBCM-8100, whose integrated hydraulic pump motor may seize due to brush wear. When the motor fails, the module disables anti-lock and stability functions and stores diagnostic trouble code C0110 for the pump motor circuit, frequently accompanied by C0265 indicating a pump motor relay circuit fault. The pump and relay circuit behavior is detailed in the [Summit Abs Service Manual :: Pump and Relay Circuit Codes](doc://man-summit-abs#diagnostic-trouble-codes/pump-and-relay-circuit-codes) and the related [Summit Abs Service Manual :: Diagnostic Trouble Codes](doc://man-summit-abs#diagnostic-trouble-codes). Base hydraulic braking is retained, but the loss of anti-lock control increases stopping distances on low-traction surfaces. Background on the affected assembly is provided in [Summit Abs Service Manual :: ABS Hydraulic Pump Assembly](doc://man-summit-abs#abs-system-overview/abs-hydraulic-pump-assembly).

# Remedy

Dealers must replace the electronic brake control module EBCM-8100 as a complete assembly, since the pump motor is not serviceable separately, following the [Summit Abs Service Manual :: ABS Module Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-summit-abs#ebcm-and-hydraulic-sub-system-service/abs-module-diagnosis-procedure) to confirm the failure before replacement. Removal and installation are covered under [Summit Abs Service Manual :: EBCM and Hydraulic Sub-System Service](doc://man-summit-abs#ebcm-and-hydraulic-sub-system-service), and module identification is described in [Summit Abs Service Manual :: Electronic Brake Control Module (EBCM)](doc://man-summit-abs#abs-system-overview/electronic-brake-control-module-ebcm). After installation the replacement module must be programmed and the system bled, then verified clear of stored codes. The repair is performed at no cost to the owner; see the [Summit Abs Service Manual](doc://man-summit-abs) for full procedure references.
