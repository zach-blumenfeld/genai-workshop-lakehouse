# Defect Description

On affected Summit vehicles, the in-tank low-pressure fuel pump (part FP-4100) may experience premature brush wear that intermittently interrupts fuel delivery to the high-pressure circuit. As supply pressure drops, the engine control module sets DTC P0087 (fuel rail/system pressure too low) and the engine may hesitate, lose power, or stall without warning, increasing crash risk. The construction and rated output of this component are described in the [Summit Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel System Overview](doc://man-summit-fuel#fuel-system-overview). A stored P0087 should be cross-checked against the [Fuel Diagnostic Trouble Code Reference](doc://man-summit-fuel#diagnosis/fuel-diagnostic-trouble-code-reference) before any parts are condemned, using the broader [Diagnosis](doc://man-summit-fuel#diagnosis) workflow.

# Remedy

Dealers will replace the in-tank fuel pump (FP-4100) free of charge following repair instruction PROC-FUEL-PUMP, which mirrors the equivalent [Fuel Pump Replacement Procedure](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery/fuel-pump-replacement-procedure) for the shared module platform. Before and after replacement, technicians must verify supply pressure and fuel trim per PROC-FUEL-DIAG, following the [Fuel Trim and Pressure Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-system-diagnosis/fuel-trim-and-pressure-diagnosis-procedure) and confirming no residual [Fuel Pressure and Sensor Faults](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-system-diagnosis/fuel-pressure-and-sensor-faults). For the underlying delivery architecture, see [Low-Pressure Fuel Pump](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery/low-pressure-fuel-pump) and the complete [Summit Fuel Service Manual](doc://man-summit-fuel).
