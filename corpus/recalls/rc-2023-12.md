title: Vega brake master cylinder seal leak reduces braking performance

# Defect Description

On affected Vega vehicles, an internal primary seal within the brake master cylinder (part BMC-7780) may swell and allow brake fluid (specification BF-7790) to bypass the piston during pedal application. This internal leak produces a slowly sinking pedal and reduced hydraulic pressure to the calipers, lengthening stopping distance and increasing crash risk. The role and service points of this component are detailed in the [Vega Brakes Service Manual :: Hydraulic Sub-System](doc://man-vega-brakes#hydraulic-sub-system) and specifically under [Master Cylinder Service](doc://man-vega-brakes#hydraulic-sub-system/master-cylinder-service). The equivalent component coverage on the shared EV platform appears in the [Marlin EV Brakes Service Manual :: Brake Master Cylinder Service](doc://man-marlin-ev-brakes#hydraulic-actuation/brake-master-cylinder-service).

# Remedy

Dealers will replace the brake master cylinder (BMC-7780) free of charge and refill the system with fresh fluid meeting specification BF-7790, following repair instruction PROC-BRAKE-FLUID. After replacement the hydraulic system must be fully flushed and bled per the [Brake Fluid Flush and Bleed](doc://man-vega-brakes#hydraulic-sub-system/brake-fluid-flush-and-bleed) steps to remove contaminated fluid and trapped air. The procedure is consistent with the [Brake Fluid Flush and Bleed Procedure](doc://man-marlin-ev-brakes#hydraulic-lines-and-fluid/brake-fluid-flush-and-bleed-procedure) under [Hydraulic Lines and Fluid](doc://man-marlin-ev-brakes#hydraulic-lines-and-fluid). For complete torque and bleed-sequence data, technicians should consult the full [Vega Brakes Service Manual](doc://man-vega-brakes).
