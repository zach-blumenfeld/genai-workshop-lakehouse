title: Harrier water pump shaft seal failure can cause coolant loss and engine overheating fire risk
area: recalls

# Defect Description

Affected Harrier engines were fitted with a water pump (part WP-6120, superseded by WP-6122) whose shaft seal can fail prematurely and allow coolant to weep past the bearing. As the WP-6120 seal degrades, progressive coolant loss reduces cooling capacity and can drive the engine into an over-temperature condition that sets diagnostic trouble code P0217. Continued operation after the P0217 condition is set risks severe overheating and an associated underhood fire hazard. Identification of affected pumps and the supersession from WP-6120 to WP-6122 is detailed in the [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Water Pump Identification and Supersession](doc://man-harrier-cooling#coolant-circulation/water-pump-identification-and-supersession), and the over-temperature diagnosis is described in the [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Thermostat and Temperature Faults](doc://man-harrier-cooling#cooling-system-diagnosis/thermostat-and-temperature-faults).

# Remedy

Dealers will replace the water pump on affected vehicles with the superseding WP-6122 unit at no cost to the owner; any remaining WP-6120 inventory must not be installed. Perform the replacement, refill, and pressure test in accordance with the [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Coolant Circulation](doc://man-harrier-cooling#coolant-circulation), then verify the P0217 condition does not return using the [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Cooling System Diagnosis](doc://man-harrier-cooling#cooling-system-diagnosis). The complete service reference is the [Harrier Cooling Service Manual](doc://man-harrier-cooling). Owners will be notified by mail and recall details are posted at https://www.nhtsa.gov/recalls.
