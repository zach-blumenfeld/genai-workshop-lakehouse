title: Heron 2.5 ignition coil insulation breakdown can cause misfire, stall, and underhood fire
area: recalls

# Defect Description

A subset of Heron 2.5 engines were assembled with ignition coils (part IC-2044-A) whose secondary winding insulation can degrade prematurely when exposed to underhood heat cycling. As the insulation on IC-2044-A breaks down, arcing across the coil produces a cylinder misfire and sets diagnostic trouble code P0351, often accompanied by rough running and stalling. In severe cases the sustained arcing can ignite nearby material and create an underhood fire risk. Diagnosis of the P0351 condition and the affected coils is covered in the [Heron Ignition Service Manual :: Coil Primary and Secondary Circuit Faults](doc://man-heron-ignition#ignition-circuit-diagnosis/coil-primary-and-secondary-circuit-faults), with application coverage detailed in the [Heron Ignition Service Manual :: Application-Specific Coils](doc://man-heron-ignition#ignition-coils/application-specific-coils).

# Remedy

Dealers will replace all ignition coils carrying part number IC-2044-A with the superseding coil at no cost to the owner. Follow the removal and installation steps in the [Heron Ignition Service Manual :: Ignition Coils](doc://man-heron-ignition#ignition-coils), confirming each cylinder fires cleanly before releasing the vehicle. After replacing IC-2044-A, clear stored codes and verify no misfire is logged using the procedures in the [Heron Ignition Service Manual :: Ignition Circuit Diagnosis](doc://man-heron-ignition#ignition-circuit-diagnosis). The complete service reference is the [Heron Ignition Service Manual](doc://man-heron-ignition), and affected owners will be notified per the recall notice at https://www.nhtsa.gov/recalls.
