# Defect Description

Affected vehicles are fitted with an electric power steering motor assembly, part number EPS-8740, whose torque sensor may produce an intermittent signal fault. When the fault is detected the steering control module disables power assist and sets diagnostic trouble code C1556 for the torque sensor circuit, resulting in a sudden increase in steering effort. The fault and its diagnosis are described in [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: EPS Torque Sensor Fault](doc://man-lumen-steering#diagnostic-trouble-codes/eps-torque-sensor-fault) and the assembly itself in [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Electric Power Steering Motor](doc://man-lumen-steering#steering-system-overview/electric-power-steering-motor). Manual steering control is retained, but the abrupt loss of assist can startle the driver, particularly at low speed.

# Remedy

Dealers must replace the electric power steering motor assembly EPS-8740 following the [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Electric Power Steering Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-lumen-steering#electric-power-steering-sub-system-service/electric-power-steering-diagnosis-procedure) to confirm the torque sensor fault before replacement, with removal and installation under [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Electric Power Steering Sub-System Service](doc://man-lumen-steering#electric-power-steering-sub-system-service). Where the repair disturbs the tie rods TROD-8700, technicians must verify and reset their position per the [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Tie Rod End Replacement Procedure](doc://man-lumen-steering#tie-rod-sub-system-service/tie-rod-end-replacement-procedure). A four-wheel alignment is required after the repair as described in [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Four-Wheel Alignment Procedure](doc://man-lumen-steering#wheel-alignment-sub-system-service/four-wheel-alignment-procedure). The complete repair is performed at no cost to the owner; see the [Lumen Steering Service Manual](doc://man-lumen-steering) for torque and calibration references.
