# Defect Description

On affected Osprey 3.5 V6 vehicles, the high-pressure fuel pump HPFP-4120 may develop a crack at the pump body seam, allowing pressurized fuel to seep into the engine compartment. As internal pressure becomes unstable, the engine control module may set low fuel rail pressure code P0087 and fuel rail pressure sensor circuit code P0190. A fuel leak in the presence of underhood ignition sources poses a fire risk, and drivers may notice a fuel odor or reduced engine power before a fault is logged. Diagnostic confirmation should follow [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Pressure and Sensor Faults](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-system-diagnosis/fuel-pressure-and-sensor-faults), with component background in [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: High-Pressure Fuel Pump (GDI)](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery/high-pressure-fuel-pump-gdi). Technicians may cross-reference the [Summit Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Diagnostic Trouble Code Reference](doc://man-summit-fuel#diagnosis/fuel-diagnostic-trouble-code-reference) for comparable P0087 and P0190 patterns.

# Remedy

Authorized dealers will replace the affected HPFP-4120 high-pressure fuel pump with a revised unit featuring a reinforced body seam, free of charge, following procedure PROC-FUEL-PUMP. Perform the replacement per [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Pump Replacement Procedure](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery/fuel-pump-replacement-procedure) and verify delivery pressure using [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel System Diagnosis](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-system-diagnosis). After installation, clear stored P0087 and P0190 codes and confirm stable fuel rail pressure during a road test; the [Summit Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Trim and Pressure Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-summit-fuel#diagnosis/fuel-trim-and-pressure-diagnosis-procedure) provides a supplementary verification reference. Owners should contact their dealer to schedule this no-charge repair.
