# Defect Description

On certain Lumen vehicles, a winding insulation defect in the electric power steering motor, part number EPS-8740, can cause an internal short under sustained high-load steering. When the controller detects the abnormal motor signal it sets DTC C1556 (EPS Torque Sensor Fault) and disables power assist to protect the system. The driver experiences a sudden increase in steering effort, which is most hazardous during low-speed parking maneuvers and tight turns. The [Electric Power Steering Motor](doc://man-lumen-steering#steering-system-overview/electric-power-steering-motor) and its operation are described in the [Steering System Overview](doc://man-lumen-steering#steering-system-overview) of the [Lumen Steering Service Manual](doc://man-lumen-steering), and the fault is detailed under [EPS Torque Sensor Fault](doc://man-lumen-steering#diagnostic-trouble-codes/eps-torque-sensor-fault).

# Remedy

Dealers will replace the EPS-8740 steering motor assembly with an improved unit free of charge and clear the stored C1556 code. Technicians must perform the [Electric Power Steering Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-lumen-steering#electric-power-steering-sub-system-service/electric-power-steering-diagnosis-procedure) to verify the fault originates in the motor and not in the torque sensor harness before replacement. Full removal and calibration steps are provided in the [Electric Power Steering Sub-System Service](doc://man-lumen-steering#electric-power-steering-sub-system-service) section. Owners should contact their dealer to arrange the repair at no cost.
