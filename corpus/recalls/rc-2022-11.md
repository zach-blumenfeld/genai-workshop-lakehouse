# Defect Description

On affected Falcon 2.0T vehicles, the in-tank low-pressure fuel pump assembly (part FP-4100) contains an impeller that can swell when exposed to certain fuel blends, increasing drag on the pump motor and reducing delivery pressure over time. As supply pressure falls below specification, the engine control module detects an out-of-range condition and sets DTC P0190, after which the engine may stall without warning and fail to restart. The failure mode and pressure thresholds match the description in the [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Low-Pressure Fuel Pump](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery/low-pressure-fuel-pump), and the P0190 diagnostic path is detailed in the [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Pressure and Sensor Faults](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-system-diagnosis/fuel-pressure-and-sensor-faults). Technicians should also review the related code reference in the [Summit Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Diagnostic Trouble Code Reference](doc://man-summit-fuel#diagnosis/fuel-diagnostic-trouble-code-reference) when confirming the fault.

# Remedy

Dealers will replace the in-tank fuel pump assembly (FP-4100) with a redesigned unit using a fuel-resistant impeller material that resists swelling across all approved fuel grades. Before and after replacement, technicians must verify delivery pressure following the [Summit Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Trim and Pressure Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-summit-fuel#diagnosis/fuel-trim-and-pressure-diagnosis-procedure) and clear DTC P0190. Refer to the [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Delivery](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery) and the [Falcon Engine Service Manual](doc://man-falcon-engine) for component access and torque specifications. This repair is performed at no charge to the owner; owners can confirm eligibility by entering their VIN at https://www.nhtsa.gov/recalls.
