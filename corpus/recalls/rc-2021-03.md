# Defect Description

Affected vehicles were assembled with high-pressure fuel pump HPFP-4120, whose pump body sealing surface may develop a hairline crack that allows pressurized fuel to weep from the GDI delivery circuit. A loss of rail pressure sets the fuel pressure performance code P0087, while a failing or out-of-range pressure sensor signal can set P0190. Fuel leaking onto hot engine components creates a fire hazard, and the loss of pressure can also cause hard starting, stalling, or a sudden loss of motive power. Diagnosis should begin with the pressure verification steps in [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Pressure and Sensor Faults](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-system-diagnosis/fuel-pressure-and-sensor-faults) and the pump-specific inspection in [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: High-Pressure Fuel Pump (GDI)](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery/high-pressure-fuel-pump-gdi).

# Remedy

Dealers will inspect and replace the affected HPFP-4120 high-pressure fuel pump with a revised pump incorporating an upgraded body seal, free of charge. The replacement and rail-pressure relief procedure must follow [Osprey Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Delivery](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery), with post-repair leak-down verification described in [Summit Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Trim and Pressure Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-summit-fuel#diagnosis/fuel-trim-and-pressure-diagnosis-procedure). After repair, confirm that P0087 and P0190 do not reset by cross-checking the values in [Summit Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel Diagnostic Trouble Code Reference](doc://man-summit-fuel#diagnosis/fuel-diagnostic-trouble-code-reference) and reviewing system layout in [Summit Fuel Service Manual :: Fuel System Overview](doc://man-summit-fuel#fuel-system-overview). Owners can verify recall status at https://www.nhtsa.gov/recalls.
