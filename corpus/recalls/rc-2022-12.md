# Defect Description

On affected Marlin EV vehicles, the HVAC blower motor assembly (part BLW-7200) draws excessive current when its brushes wear unevenly, causing the motor circuit and connector to overheat. The thermal stress can melt the connector housing, produce cabin smoke, and in severe cases create a fire risk while the climate system is running. The body control module detects the abnormal circuit condition and sets DTC B1352, which is described in the [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor Circuit Code](doc://man-heron-hvac#diagnostic-trouble-codes/blower-motor-circuit-code) and confirmed through the [Marlin EV Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor Circuit Fault Diagnosis](doc://man-marlin-ev-hvac#hvac-system-diagnosis/blower-motor-circuit-fault-diagnosis). Technicians should reference the [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Blower Motor and Cabin Air Filter](doc://man-heron-hvac#hvac-system-overview/blower-motor-and-cabin-air-filter) to confirm component identification before service.

# Remedy

Dealers will replace the HVAC blower motor (BLW-7200) and its connector with a revised assembly that uses higher-temperature insulation and balanced brushes to prevent current overdraw. Replacement must follow the [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: HVAC Blower Motor Replacement Procedure](doc://man-heron-hvac#air-distribution-sub-system-service/hvac-blower-motor-replacement-procedure), after which technicians clear DTC B1352 and verify normal blower operation across all speeds. Supporting air distribution details are provided in the [Heron Hvac Service Manual :: Air Distribution Sub-System Service](doc://man-heron-hvac#air-distribution-sub-system-service) and the [Marlin EV Hvac Service Manual :: HVAC System Diagnosis](doc://man-marlin-ev-hvac#hvac-system-diagnosis). This repair is performed at no charge to the owner; owners can confirm eligibility by entering their VIN at https://www.nhtsa.gov/recalls.
