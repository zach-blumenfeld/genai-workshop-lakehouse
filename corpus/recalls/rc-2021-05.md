# Defect Description

Affected vehicles were built with water pump WP-6120, whose mechanical shaft seal may fail prematurely and allow coolant to escape from the pump housing. The resulting loss of coolant circulation can cause the engine to overheat, setting the engine over-temperature code P0217 and, if the condition is ignored, leading to engine damage and a risk of fire. Diagnosis of the over-temperature condition should follow [Lumen Cooling Service Manual :: P0217 Engine Over-Temperature Condition](doc://man-lumen-cooling#diagnostic-trouble-codes/p0217-engine-over-temperature-condition) together with the inspection steps in [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Thermostat and Temperature Faults](doc://man-harrier-cooling#cooling-system-diagnosis/thermostat-and-temperature-faults). Technicians should confirm coolant flow and seal weep using [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Cooling System Diagnosis](doc://man-harrier-cooling#cooling-system-diagnosis).

# Remedy

Dealers will replace the affected WP-6120 water pump with the superseding pump WP-6122, which incorporates an upgraded shaft seal, free of charge. The supersession and correct part selection are documented in [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Water Pump Identification and Supersession](doc://man-harrier-cooling#coolant-circulation/water-pump-identification-and-supersession), and the replacement must follow [Lumen Cooling Service Manual :: Water Pump Replacement Procedure](doc://man-lumen-cooling#water-pump-sub-system/water-pump-replacement-procedure). After installing the WP-6122 pump, refill and bleed the coolant circuit per [Harrier Cooling Service Manual :: Coolant Circulation](doc://man-harrier-cooling#coolant-circulation) and confirm that P0217 does not reset during a heat-soak road test. Owners can verify recall status at https://www.nhtsa.gov/recalls.
