# Defect Description

On affected Osprey 3.5 V6 vehicles, the high-pressure fuel pump, part number HPFP-4120, uses an internal seal that can degrade with exposure to ethanol-blended fuel. As the seal hardens it loses sealing pressure, which causes a measurable drop in rail pressure and sets DTC P0087 (Fuel Rail/System Pressure Too Low). A degraded seal can also weep fuel onto hot engine components, creating an underhood fire risk. The [High-Pressure Fuel Pump (GDI)](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery/high-pressure-fuel-pump-gdi) is documented in the [Fuel Delivery](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-delivery) chapter of the [Osprey Fuel Service Manual](doc://man-osprey-fuel), with diagnostic detail under [Fuel Pressure and Sensor Faults](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-system-diagnosis/fuel-pressure-and-sensor-faults).

# Remedy

Dealers will replace the HPFP-4120 high-pressure fuel pump with a revised assembly using an ethanol-resistant seal at no charge. Before condemning the pump, technicians should follow the [Fuel System Diagnosis](doc://man-osprey-fuel#fuel-system-diagnosis) routine to confirm P0087 is caused by the pump rather than a clogged filter or failed pressure sensor. After replacement the rail must be pressure-tested for leaks and the trouble code cleared. Owners should contact their dealer to schedule the free repair as soon as possible given the fire risk.
