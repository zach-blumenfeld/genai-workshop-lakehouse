title: Safety Recall - Lower Control Arm Fracture Loss of Control
area: recalls
---

# Defect Description

Affected vehicles may have a lower control arm, part number CARM-8540, manufactured with an inconsistent weld at the rear bushing mount that can develop a fatigue crack under sustained load. If the crack propagates, the control arm can fracture, allowing the wheel to deflect and producing a sudden loss of directional control. The role of this component is described in [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Control Arms and Sway Bar Links](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#suspension-system-overview/control-arms-and-sway-bar-links) and the broader [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Suspension System Overview](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#suspension-system-overview). Owners may notice a clunk or wandering steering as an early indicator of a developing crack.

# Remedy

Dealers must replace the affected lower control arm CARM-8540 on both sides following the [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Control Arm Replacement Procedure](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#control-arm-and-sway-bar-sub-system-service/control-arm-replacement-procedure) within the [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Control Arm and Sway Bar Sub-System Service](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#control-arm-and-sway-bar-sub-system-service). Because the control arm carries the wheel hub bearing WHB-8580, technicians must inspect that bearing for any play or damage incurred during the fracture event using the [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Wheel Hub Bearing Replacement Procedure](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#wheel-hub-bearing-sub-system-service/wheel-hub-bearing-replacement-procedure) and replace it if necessary. A four-wheel alignment must be performed after the repair, and the full procedure set is referenced in the [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension). All work is performed at no charge to the owner.
