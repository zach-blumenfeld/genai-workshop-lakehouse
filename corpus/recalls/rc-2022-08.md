id: rc-2022-08
title: Vega front brake caliper piston seizure can cause brake drag, overheating, and reduced braking
area: recalls
---

# Defect Description

Certain Vega vehicles were built with front brake calipers (part BCAL-7760) whose piston bore can corrode and cause the piston to seize in the applied position. A seized BCAL-7760 caliper keeps the front brake pads (part BP-7720) in constant contact with the rotor, producing brake drag, excessive heat, and premature wear of BP-7720. Prolonged dragging degrades braking performance and, in extreme cases, can damage adjacent components. Inspection criteria for the front corner are described in the [Vega Brakes Service Manual :: Front Brake Sub-System](doc://man-vega-brakes#front-brake-sub-system) and the [Vega Brakes Service Manual :: Caliper Sub-System](doc://man-vega-brakes#caliper-sub-system).

# Remedy

Dealers will replace the affected front caliper BCAL-7760 and the associated front brake pads BP-7720 at no charge to the owner. Replace the caliper following the [Vega Brakes Service Manual :: Brake Caliper Replacement](doc://man-vega-brakes#caliper-sub-system/brake-caliper-replacement), then service the friction material per the [Vega Brakes Service Manual :: Front Brake Pad Replacement](doc://man-vega-brakes#front-brake-sub-system/front-brake-pad-replacement). After installing BCAL-7760 and BP-7720, bleed the system and confirm the pads release freely with no residual drag, referencing the [Vega Brakes Service Manual :: Front Brake Pad and Rotor Service](doc://man-vega-brakes#front-brake-sub-system/front-brake-pad-and-rotor-service). The complete repair reference is the [Vega Brakes Service Manual](doc://man-vega-brakes); owners will be notified by mail and recall information is available at https://www.nhtsa.gov/recalls.
