title: Marlin EV front brake hose may chafe against suspension component, causing fluid leak and loss of braking
area: recalls

# Defect Description

On affected Marlin EV vehicles, the front brake hose BH-7745 can contact a nearby suspension component through the steering range, causing the hose outer layer to chafe and eventually wear through. A compromised BH-7745 can develop a fluid leak that reduces hydraulic pressure to the front brakes. Technicians should inspect the routing and condition of the hose using the [Marlin EV Brakes Service Manual :: Hydraulic Lines and Fluid](doc://man-marlin-ev-brakes#hydraulic-lines-and-fluid) section. Comparable inspection guidance for the front circuit is available in the [Vega Brakes Service Manual :: Front Brake Sub-System](doc://man-vega-brakes#front-brake-sub-system).

# Remedy

Dealers will replace the front brake hose BH-7745 with a revised hose featuring corrected routing and additional standoff clearance, following the [Marlin EV Brakes Service Manual :: Brake Hose Replacement Procedure](doc://man-marlin-ev-brakes#hydraulic-lines-and-fluid/brake-hose-replacement-procedure). After the hose is replaced, the system must be refilled with brake fluid BF-7790 and bled to remove air per the [Marlin EV Brakes Service Manual :: Brake Fluid Flush and Bleed Procedure](doc://man-marlin-ev-brakes#hydraulic-lines-and-fluid/brake-fluid-flush-and-bleed-procedure). Technicians may also reference the equivalent [Vega Brakes Service Manual :: Brake Fluid Flush and Bleed](doc://man-vega-brakes#hydraulic-sub-system/brake-fluid-flush-and-bleed) and [Vega Brakes Service Manual :: Front Brake Hose Replacement](doc://man-vega-brakes#front-brake-sub-system/front-brake-hose-replacement) procedures for technique. This recall remedy is performed at no charge; see https://www.nhtsa.gov/recalls for owner notification details.
