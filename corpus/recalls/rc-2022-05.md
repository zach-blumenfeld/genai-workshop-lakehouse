# Defect Description

On certain Falcon vehicles, a manufacturing defect in the alternator rectifier, part number ALT-8810, can develop an internal short that overheats the charging circuit. When the engine control module detects abnormal generator output it sets DTC P0620 (Generator Control Circuit Malfunction) and the battery warning lamp illuminates. A sustained short can discharge the battery and cause the engine to stall, and in the worst case the overheated rectifier can scorch surrounding wiring and create a fire risk. The [Alternator](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-overview/alternator) is described in the [Charging System Overview](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-overview) of the [Falcon Charging Service Manual](doc://man-falcon-charging), and the code is listed under [Generator Control Circuit Code](doc://man-falcon-charging#diagnostic-trouble-codes/generator-control-circuit-code).

# Remedy

Dealers will replace the ALT-8810 alternator with a redesigned unit at no charge to the owner. Technicians must run the [Charging System Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-falcon-charging#charging-system-diagnosis-sub-system/charging-system-diagnosis-procedure) to confirm P0620 originates in the alternator and not the field control wiring before replacement. The removal and installation steps are provided in the [Alternator Replacement Procedure](doc://man-falcon-charging#alternator-sub-system-service/alternator-replacement-procedure) under the [Alternator Sub-System Service](doc://man-falcon-charging#alternator-sub-system-service) section. Owners should contact their dealer to arrange the free repair promptly.
