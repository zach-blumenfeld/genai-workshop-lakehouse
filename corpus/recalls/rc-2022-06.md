title: Ridgeline HD lower control arm weld defect may allow separation and loss of vehicle control

# Defect Description

Affected Ridgeline HD trucks were built with lower control arms (part CARM-8540) whose front cross-member weld may have been incompletely fused during fabrication. Under sustained suspension loading the deficient weld on CARM-8540 can propagate a crack and ultimately allow the control arm to separate at the mounting point, leading to a sudden loss of steering control. Symptoms preceding failure typically include a pronounced front-end clunk and accelerated, uneven tire wear, as documented in [Ridgeline HD: Front clunk and uneven tire wear from worn lower control arm :: Condition](doc://tsb-20-508#condition). Technicians should review the broader system context in the [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Control Arms and Sway Bar Links](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#suspension-system-overview/control-arms-and-sway-bar-links) before beginning any inspection.

# Remedy

Dealers will inspect and replace the lower control arm CARM-8540 on all affected vehicles at no charge, regardless of the part's apparent condition. Perform the replacement following the [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual :: Control Arm Replacement Procedure](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension#control-arm-and-sway-bar-sub-system-service/control-arm-replacement-procedure), torquing all fasteners to the published specification and verifying alignment afterward. The full repair sequence and supporting reference material are contained in the [Ridgeline HD Suspension Service Manual](doc://man-ridgeline-hd-suspension), and the related condition report in [Ridgeline HD: Front clunk and uneven tire wear from worn lower control arm](doc://tsb-20-508) should be closed out concurrently. Owners of affected vehicles will be notified by mail; recall details are also posted at https://www.nhtsa.gov/recalls.
