# Defect Description

Certain Lumen vehicles were assembled with an electric power steering motor, part number EPS-8740, whose torque-sensor signal can drift out of calibration over time. When the drift exceeds the controller threshold the EPS module sets diagnostic trouble code C1556 and commands a fail-safe that drops steering assist. The [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Electric Power Steering Motor](doc://man-lumen-steering#steering-system-overview/electric-power-steering-motor) describes the motor and integrated sensor, and the fault behavior is detailed under [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: EPS Torque Sensor Fault](doc://man-lumen-steering#diagnostic-trouble-codes/eps-torque-sensor-fault).

# Safety Risk and Affected Vehicles

A sudden loss of power steering assist from EPS-8740 substantially increases steering effort and can lead to a crash, particularly during low-speed maneuvers and parking. Affected vehicles are Lumen units within the identified build range; confirm the steering configuration using the [Lumen Steering Service Manual](doc://man-lumen-steering) and the [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Steering System Overview](doc://man-lumen-steering#steering-system-overview) before beginning work.

# Remedy

Dealers will reflash and recalibrate the EPS module and, where the torque sensor cannot be brought into specification, replace the motor assembly EPS-8740 at no charge under procedure PROC-EPS-DIAG. Technicians should clear C1556, then validate the repair following the [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Electric Power Steering Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-lumen-steering#electric-power-steering-sub-system-service/electric-power-steering-diagnosis-procedure) within the [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Diagnostic Trouble Codes](doc://man-lumen-steering#diagnostic-trouble-codes) framework. Owners may check whether their vehicle is included and find remedy details at https://www.nhtsa.gov/recalls.
