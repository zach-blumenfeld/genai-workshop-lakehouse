title: Osprey outer tie rod end may separate causing loss of steering

# Defect Description

On affected Osprey vehicles, the outer tie rod end (part TROD-8700) may corrode at the ball-stud joint and separate from the steering knuckle during normal operation. When separation occurs, the driver experiences an immediate and total loss of directional control on the affected side, dramatically increasing the risk of a crash. In some cases the electronic steering control module logs DTC C1234 prior to physical separation as the steering angle signal becomes erratic. Technicians can review the diagnostic context for this fault in the [Lumen Steering Service Manual :: Diagnostic Trouble Codes](doc://man-lumen-steering#diagnostic-trouble-codes) and correlate it against the [Steering Angle Sensor Fault](doc://man-lumen-steering#diagnostic-trouble-codes/steering-angle-sensor-fault) entry. Background on how the linkage is constructed is documented under [Steering Rack and Tie Rods](doc://man-lumen-steering#steering-system-overview/steering-rack-and-tie-rods).

# Remedy

Dealers will inspect and replace the outer tie rod end (TROD-8700) on both sides of the vehicle free of charge, following the supplied repair instruction PROC-TIE-ROD. The replacement must be performed in accordance with the [Tie Rod End Replacement Procedure](doc://man-lumen-steering#tie-rod-sub-system-service/tie-rod-end-replacement-procedure) within the [Tie Rod Sub-System Service](doc://man-lumen-steering#tie-rod-sub-system-service) section. After any tie rod end is disturbed, a four-wheel alignment is mandatory per PROC-ALIGN; technicians must complete the [Four-Wheel Alignment Procedure](doc://man-lumen-steering#wheel-alignment-sub-system-service/four-wheel-alignment-procedure) to restore correct toe and steering centering. Owners may also consult the full [Lumen Steering Service Manual](doc://man-lumen-steering) for torque specifications and post-repair verification steps.
