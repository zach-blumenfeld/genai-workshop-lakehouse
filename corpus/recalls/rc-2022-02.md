id: rc-2022-02
title: Summit ABS/EBCM control module solder joint cracking disables anti-lock braking and extends stopping distance
area: recalls
---

# Defect Description

On affected Summit vehicles, repeated thermal cycling can fracture the internal solder joints of the Electronic Brake Control Module, part number EBCM-8100. When a joint cracks, the module loses the continuity needed to drive the pump and relay circuit and will set DTC C0265, a pump motor circuit fault that disables anti-lock function. In severe cases the module can no longer communicate over the vehicle bus, setting U0121 (Lost Communication with ABS Control Module) and illuminating the ABS warning lamp. The [Electronic Brake Control Module (EBCM)](doc://man-summit-abs#abs-system-overview/electronic-brake-control-module-ebcm) is described in the [Summit Abs Service Manual](doc://man-summit-abs), which also documents the relevant [Pump and Relay Circuit Codes](doc://man-summit-abs#diagnostic-trouble-codes/pump-and-relay-circuit-codes).

# Safety Risk

With anti-lock braking disabled, a hard stop on a low-traction surface can cause wheel lockup and loss of directional control, extending stopping distance and increasing crash risk. The driver retains conventional hydraulic braking, but the loss of ABS modulation is not always obvious until an emergency stop is attempted. Because the [System Configuration and Communication Codes](doc://man-summit-abs#diagnostic-trouble-codes/system-configuration-and-communication-codes) may set intermittently, the fault can appear and clear before the operator recognizes the hazard. Refer to the full [Diagnostic Trouble Codes](doc://man-summit-abs#diagnostic-trouble-codes) chapter for the complete code list.

# Remedy

Dealers will inspect the affected module and replace the EBCM-8100 control module with a revised unit that uses a reinforced solder process at no charge to the owner. Technicians must follow the [ABS Module Diagnosis Procedure](doc://man-summit-abs#ebcm-and-hydraulic-sub-system-service/abs-module-diagnosis-procedure) before condemning the part to confirm that C0265 and U0121 are not caused by upstream wiring or connector corrosion. After replacement, the module must be reconfigured and the hydraulic unit bled per the [EBCM and Hydraulic Sub-System Service](doc://man-summit-abs#ebcm-and-hydraulic-sub-system-service) instructions. Owners should contact their dealer to schedule the free repair.
