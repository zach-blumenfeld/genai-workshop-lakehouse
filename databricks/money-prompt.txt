THE POINT: "patterns across ALL our bulletins and recalls" asks you to COVER and

structured layer over the docs is what makes coverage + clustering reliable. (The
question also can't collapse to SQL -- the warehouse has no doc text.)

------------------------------ RESULT A = DATABRICKS (AI Search + Genie) -------
I've now mapped the bulletins and recalls against the fleet. Here's what emerges.

How the two sources fit together
- Bulletins & recalls live in the AI Search doc index (recalls/rc-*.pdf,
  bulletins/tsb-*.pdf) -- there is no recall/bulletin table in Genie.
- The fleet lives in Genie (autofix_service.vehicles). It's perfectly uniform:
  exactly 100 cars per model+engine variant (1,700 vehicles total). So car counts
  below come straight from which model/engine each document scopes to.

A key meta-pattern first: recalls and bulletins repeatedly describe the same
failing part and DTC, just at different severities. The recall is the safety-defect
version (free replacement, superseded part); the bulletin is the high-mileage
wear-out version of the identical component. You see this on the fuel pump, water
pump, EPS, and ignition coil. Three failure families also share a leak-to-fire
mechanism (fuel, coolant, wiring), and almost every one ships a superseded part
(WP-6120->6122, FP-4100 redesigned, HPFP-4120 revised seal, IC-2044-A revised winding).

------------------------------ RESULT B = GRAPH (connections) ------------------
The library is 76 bulletins + 36 recalls over 2020-2025, and they aren't 112 unique
problems -- they're ~12 recurring failure modes, each re-issued almost every
model-year as a fresh TSB, with the worst ones escalating into a safety recall.
Bulletins and recalls are the same patterns at two severities. Each pattern is tied
to one model line (occasionally two), so "cars affected" is that line's fleet.

Fleet: 1,700 cars, 100 per model+engine (Falcon/Harrier/Kestrel/Lumen/Marlin EV/
Summit/Vega = 200 each across two engines; Heron/Osprey/Ridgeline HD = 100).
